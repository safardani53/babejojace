The best shower cleaners for 2022, according to a cleaning expert

BLITZING your bathroom? We've 'scoured' the shops and spoken to the experts to find the best shower cleaners money can buy.

Tough-to-shift mould and limescale can make it easy to put off your cleaning day, but thankfully the right product will have your shower sparkling clean in no time.

It's easy to think that our shower is one of the cleanest spaces in our home, but if you're not armed with one of the best shower cleaners on the market, it can be a haven for germs.

It’s one of the most bacteria-ridden spaces in the home: researchers have found that shower heads contain mycobacteria, which can cause lung infections. Yuck.

To counteract, you'll need a shower cleaner that can remove mould and limescale, as well as something that will help descale those tiles from grout and clean stainless steel fixtures like the shower head. Meanwhile, a squeegee is the most effective tool for keeping shower glass clean, without needing to use super-potent chemicals like bleach.

Ready to channel your inner Mrs Hinch? We've rounded up some of the best shower cleaners on the market below.

Best shower cleaners at a glance:

How to clean your shower, according to an expert

According to Nicola Rodriguez AKA Essex House Dolly, a cleaning expert with decades of experience making homes spic-and-span, the first step in cleaning your shower might sound obvious, but it often isn't followed.

1. Empty the shower

When you first tackle cleaning your shower, always totally empty it," she tells Sun Selects. "This may seem common sense, but many people often clean around things in their shower. Your showering and hair products can often accumulate mould around the bottoms and a lot of gunk around the top, so these need a thorough clean."

She also recommends throwing away products such as razors and sponges regularly (every two weeks, in the case of sponges), as they can be breeding grounds for bacteria.

2. Clean the plughole

After the shower has been emptied, she recommends tackling the plughole.

It is amazing how so many people do not clean their plug", she says. "They think pouring an unblocker product just magics the horrors away — it doesn't".

"I have sticks that you simply stick down the plughole and when you pull them up, they gip all the nasty hairs, grime and other unwanted disgusting things out. It is astonishing what comes up!"

She then uses Mr Muscle Plughole Unblocker, pouring boiling water first to loosen any gunk — beware of fumes, though!

3. Get rid of black mould

Once the plughole is clean, turn your attention to the rest of the shower.

"Using Cillit Bang Black Mould Remover, spray any areas of the shower where you can black mould spots appearing", says Nicola. "This can then soak in while you continue cleaning the rest of the shower".

4. Clean the glass

While the Cillit Bang is working its magic, Nicola says to get to work on your shower's glass.

"Use Dettol Bathroom Spray over the shower glass walls and also around the tap section. Once applied, using a sponge with an abrasive side, in circle motions, go over the areas. Once completed, shower off the Dettol, as well as the Cillit Bang Black mould remover."

5. Get rid of any stubborn marks

Once the majority of the shower is clean, it's time to get rid of any stubborn stains. To do this, Nicola uses The Pink Stuff Miracle Cleaning Paste, which can also be used in many other areas of your house, including cooker tops, sinks and barbecues.

"Vikal is also handy if you have a build-up of limescale," she says. "At home, I tend to use this product overnight on taps and wrap kitchen roll around it."

6. Clean the shower head

"You should also make sure that you clean your shower heads regularly," says Nicola. "I fill a carrier bag with white vinegar and Zoflora then tie the bag around the shower head or leave it in the sink. Leave it for around an hour and then let the water flush it through."

7. Get rid of water marks

"One of my bugbears, being someone who has OCD, is water marks — I absolutely loathe them," says Nicola. "I combat this by using microfibre cloths and a spray of white vinegar."

First, lightly spray the vinegar over wet glass, before using one microfibre cloth to dry the surfaces. Then use a second cloth to go over the dry glass, as well as the tap and shower tray.

If you find the smell of white vinegar offputting, she recommends using a scented disinfectant to clean the floor of the shower — her favourite is Zoflora Fig & Cedar.

How do I get rid of shower grime?

In addition to using shower cleaners like Viakal to get rid of grime build-up and limescale, you can also try a DIY remedy to clean the shower of grime.

Try mixing white vinegar and hot water in equal amounts, then use a washcloth or sponge to clean.

For really stubborn mould, a natural alternative like white vinegar may work – just leave it to sit for up to 60 minutes.

However, you're likely to need something stronger, like bleach. Or try Cillit Bang Black Mould Remover, which thousands of fans swear by.

Do steam cleaners work on showers?

Pressurised steam cleaners are great for just about any room in the home, including bathrooms and showers.

Steam combats and breaks down stubborn dirt, grout and grime, sanitising them with just hot air alone.

Can you use drain cleaner in the shower?

Drain cleaner will work to unclog shower plugs of build-up dirt, soap and hair but it's often laden with toxic chemicals.

You're best to make your own homemade cleaner from cupboard staples - including simple baking soda and vinegar.

First, pour boiling water down your shower drain, then pour a cup (around 250ml) of baking soda, followed by a cup of vinegar down the drain.

Then, wait a couple of hours before following up with boiling water - and voila!
